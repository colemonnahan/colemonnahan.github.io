About me

I grew up in near Seattle, WA and studied Mathematics and German Language at Western Washington University in Bellingham, WA. After that I worked for a few years in a USDA dairy chemistry lab as an Agricultural Statistician. While I enjoyed my time there, I wanted to broaden my education and skillset and work on problems that lie in the intersection of quantitative sciences, ecology, and resource management.

Lacking the skills necessary to pursue such a career, I decided to go to graduate school and found the QERM interdisciplinary graduate program at the UW to be the ideal place to develop them. After a year of rigorous training I joined the Trevor Branch's lab where I completed my MS and PhD degrees.
